Sitamarhi is a culturally rich town in the state of Bihar, India, revered as the birthplace of Goddess Sita from the epic Ramayana. The region is deeply rooted in spiritual heritage, traditional values, and ancient mythology. Pilgrims and visitors are drawn to its sacred temples, vibrant festivals, and peaceful rural landscape that reflects the timeless essence of Mithila culture.

Sitamarhi is part of the Mithila cultural region, known for its vibrant traditions, Maithili language, and devotional art forms. Festivals like Vivah Panchami, Ram Navami, and Chhath Puja are celebrated with great devotion and community participation. Traditional folk songs, rituals, and temple processions reflect the spiritual identity of the region.
